# 10-Slide Presentation Outline: Customer Segmentation Using Machine Learning

## Slide 1: Problem Statement
Retail marketing teams need a data-driven way to group customers for personalized campaigns, retention, and promotion planning.

## Slide 2: Dataset Overview
Synthetic dataset with 6,000 customers covering demographics, income, location, product preference, spending, purchase frequency, recency, and online/store behavior.

## Slide 3: EDA Findings
Customer value varies strongly by income, purchase frequency, product category, and recency. Spending and frequency show clear behavioral separation.

## Slide 4: Feature Engineering
Created Customer_Value_Score, Purchase_Intensity, Recency_Score, Engagement_Score, and RFM metrics.

## Slide 5: Clustering Approach
Standardized features with StandardScaler and compared K-Means, hierarchical clustering, and DBSCAN.

## Slide 6: Model Evaluation
Used silhouette score, Davies-Bouldin index, and Calinski-Harabasz score. K-Means diagnostics included elbow curve and silhouette analysis.

## Slide 7: Segment Profiles
Segments are automatically named based on spending, frequency, recency, and online behavior.

## Slide 8: Business Insights
High-value customers contribute disproportionate revenue, recent frequent buyers have strong retention potential, and stale low-value customers require win-back campaigns.

## Slide 9: Recommendations
Launch VIP programs, digital-first campaigns, value promotions, and lifecycle retention journeys matched to each segment.

## Slide 10: Conclusion
Machine learning segmentation converts raw customer data into actionable targeting groups that can improve retention, campaign ROI, and customer lifetime value.
